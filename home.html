AVL TREES

What are AVL trees?

Named after their inventor Adelson, Velski & Landis, AVL trees are height balancing binary search tree. AVL tree checks the height of the left and the right sub-trees and assures that the difference is not more than 1. This difference is called the Balance Factor.

Operations

Insertion:Adding an element to an AVL tree is similar to adding one in a BST, but the process takes into account an extra attribute, the balance factor for each node. This factor indicates whether the tree is left-heavy (the height of the left sub-tree is 1 greater than the right sub-tree), balanced (both sub-trees are the same height) or right-heavy (the height of the right sub-tree is 1 greater than the left sub-tree). If the balance would be destroyed by an insertion, a rotation is performed to correct the balance.
